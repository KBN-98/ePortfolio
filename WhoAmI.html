Who am I?

What do these results mean to me?
The results are a good indicator of my strengths, weaknesses, and my priorities. The MBTI results declare that I am driven by curiosity and ask plenty of questions. Which is true, I frequently research for fun; if a topic piques my interest, I often am down a Wikipedia article rabbit hole before I know it. While I do not agree with everything it claimed, the MBTI type I was given has given me the opportunity to self-reflect on my thoughts and ideals.
The learning styles chart has not provided me with much clarity as I had hoped, with the of the results returning the exact same value. What I can take from this is that hopefully, I am equally engaged with all kinds of learning styles.
I am pleasantly surprised by the result of this leadership style quiz; I can strongly relate to each of the qualities they described. I strive to be both pragmatic and innovative while holding onto my authentic self. I believe the best way to manage a team is by empowering your team through both your own self-confidence and a genuine connection with them.

How do these affect my teamwork?
My MBTI type of ‘Turbulent Debater’ I think is a crucial component to any team. I can use my social engagement strategy to challenge my team members in back and forth debate; this is a great way to analyse different approaches promptly.
My ideals as a ‘Metamodern Leader’ also come to play in this sense, balancing idealism, and pragmatism; I can push the team to new and better ideas. On the other hand, my prospecting nature can lead to forgetting details and impulsive decision making. I would need to keep in mind not to let my ‘hard and fast’ personality get the better of me in team decision making.

How will take this into account while forming a team?
I tend to overthink and find it hard to focus or even focus on the wrong task at times. My turbulent personality leads to bouts of self-doubt and insecurity, so I think another strong individual to help drive progress would benefit me in a team.
I believe the qualities I have would benefit from other personality types to balance the energy I bring. I tend to get very driven and passionate in my work, but usually in short bursts. While I can push the team to explore innovation, I would need another team member able to keep me grounded to reality, and gentle reminders to consider other peers’ feelings in the process.
